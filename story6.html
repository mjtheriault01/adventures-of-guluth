Story 6

The City of Cheese

For as long as any mouse could remember, there was a legend whispered in the cozy streets of Brie-Borough. It was the story of the City of Cheese — a mythical place where the mountains were made of aged gouda and the waterfalls flowed with liquid gold. Some said it wasn't real, but Guluth had spent many long nights studying old maps. He believed the city was out there, and he believed its treasure was meant to be shared with everyone who was hungry.

But Guluth wasn't the only one looking. Two greedy explorer rats named Ferdinand and Mellin were following his trail. They didn't want to share; they wanted to claim the city for themselves and become the richest rats in history.

Guluth's journey led him to a deep, stone cavern. On the walls were ancient carvings: "Those who seek the greatest treasure may enter, but enter at your own risk. For if you take only for yourself, you may never return."

Guluth read the words with care. He knew that to find the city, he had to keep his heart true.

Inside the tunnel, he faced many obstacles. There were swinging blades that he had to dive under — whoosh! — and a massive, dark abyss. Guluth saw a single rope hanging over the drop.

Dad's Question

Guluth is a very small mouse and the abyss is very deep. Do you think he's brave enough to swing across?

With a calm breath, Guluth grabbed the rope and swung across. He landed lightly on his paws, but Ferdinand and Mellin were right on his tail, scurrying past the traps Guluth had already navigated.

Soon, the air began to smell sweet and savory. Guluth reached a river, but it wasn't a river of water. It was a river of melted, golden cheese! He hopped onto a small wooden boat and drifted quietly down the stream.

Suddenly, he rounded a corner and saw it: The City of Cheese.

Buildings rose like golden towers. Mountains of aged cheese touched the clouds. There was a small sign in a field of crackers that said: "Eat what you need, but touch nothing else." Guluth took a small, polite bite — it was the best cheese he had ever tasted.

But then, he heard the splashing of oars. Ferdinand and Mellin arrived in their boat, their eyes wide with greed.

"Haha! Little Guluth!" Ferdinand shouted. "We followed you here, and now this city is ours! We're going to take it all!"

"I cannot let you do that," Guluth said calmly. "This cheese is for everyone — mice and rats alike. If you take it all and waste it, there will be nothing left for those who are hungry."

"Who's going to stop us?" Mellin sneered.

Guluth looked at the thick, sticky river of cheese. "You are going to stop yourselves," he replied. "But if you think you are faster, let's have a race. A swimming contest across the Cheese River to the city gates. If you win, you can try to take the cheese. If I win, you must leave and never return."

The rats laughed. "Rats are much faster swimmers than mice!" they cheered.

They dove into the golden river. But Guluth's clever mind had already solved the puzzle. The melted cheese was thick and sticky. Because the rats were large and heavy, they began to get bogged down, their long limbs getting stuck in the gooey cheddar. But Guluth was a small, light mouse. He moved quickly and easily over the surface, skimming across while the rats struggled behind him.

Guluth reached the shore first. He didn't brag; he simply waited for them to climb out. "Now, return home," he said firmly but kindly.

The rats were embarrassed and scurried away, but Guluth knew they might try to return with others. So, he stayed at the city gates and began to build. He used the environment to create giant cheese-slides and messy cheese-waterfalls.

When any greedy rats eventually tried to return, they found themselves slipping and sliding away from the hidden entrance, unable to get past Guluth's clever defenses.

Eventually, the rats realized they could never outsmart the clever mouse. Guluth then traveled back to Brie-Borough and gathered his friends. Together, they brought back just enough cheese to ensure that every family in Mouseland had a full belly.

Guluth sat on a high hill overlooking the city he had protected. He was just a small mouse with a single purple birthmark, but he had saved the greatest treasure in the world by making sure it was shared.

"I am Guluth," he whispered, looking up at the stars, "and I am the guardian of the light."

The End.
