Story 2

The Good Neighbor

After Guluth became a member of the family in the little house, he began to travel. He wanted to see the world and find the very best cheeses for his friends. One day, his journey took him to the eastern side of the great Swiss Mountains, where the air was crisp and the peaks were capped with white snow like frosting on a cake.

As Guluth walked along the dusty road, he saw something tucked in the tall grass. It was a rat named Harriet. Poor Harriet had been traveling alone when two mean rats ambushed her. They had taken her food, her supplies, and pushed her into the dirt before scurrying away.

Now, in those days, mice and rats didn't usually get along. Most mice would see a rat and run as fast as their little legs could carry them! Rats were big, and rats could be scary.

A rat named Horus came walking down the road next. He saw Harriet lying there, but he just shook his head. "Not my problem," Horus muttered, and he kept right on walking.

But then came Guluth.

Dad's Question

Most mice are afraid of rats. What do you think Guluth will do?

Guluth didn't run. He didn't hide. He saw someone in trouble, and that was all that mattered. He walked right up to Harriet.

"Are you okay, little rat?" Guluth asked softly.

He didn't wait for an answer. He reached into his small blue satchel and pulled out a tiny flask of fresh water. He helped Harriet take a sip. Then, he pulled out a piece of his very special Swiss mountain cheese — the best he had found on his whole trip — and gave it to her.

Harriet looked at him with big, surprised eyes. "Thank you," she whispered. "Who are you?"

"My name is Guluth," he said. "I am just a mouse, but I couldn't leave you here on the side of the road."

"But mice don't help rats," Harriet said. "Rats are usually mean to mice. Why would you risk your cheese and your safety for me?"

Guluth looked at the snowy mountains and then back at Harriet. "Because even though you are a rat and I am a mouse, you are still important. You have value, Harriet. And because of that, I will help you."

Guluth stayed with her until she was strong enough to stand. Then, he walked beside her all the way back to her village. When they arrived, Guluth saw hundreds of rats. They had long tails and sharp teeth, and when they saw a tiny mouse walking into their town, they began to growl and stare.

"You should go, Guluth," Harriet whispered nervously. "I'm afraid they might hurt you."

"Don't worry about me, Harriet," Guluth said with a calm smile. "I have a plan."

Suddenly, the crowd of rats parted, and out stepped the meanest, scariest rat of them all. His name was Rondon. Rondon was the "Town Mean Rat," and he wore a jagged tooth around his neck.

"Well, well," Rondon sneered, looking down at Guluth. "A little mouse has wandered into my village. I think I'll eat you and take that special cheese you're carrying."

Guluth didn't flinch. He looked up at the giant rat and said, "Ah, Rondon! I have heard of you. Stories of your greatness travel far and wide — even to the eastern side of the Swiss Mountains and the western side of the Alps!"

Rondon stopped. He blinked his beady eyes. "You have? People are talking about my... greatness?"

"Oh, yes," Guluth said, using his clever mind. He knew that Rondon was very proud and loved to be talked about. "I've heard you are the most powerful rat in the land. In fact, I heard you wouldn't even bother with a tiny mouse like me. A rat as great as you would want a much bigger feast!"

Rondon puffed out his chest. "Well... you're right. I am pretty great. And you are quite small."

"Exactly," Guluth said. "In fact, if you follow me over that hill and behind the waterfall, I know where a truly magnificent feast is hidden. A rat of your importance deserves nothing less."

Greed and pride filled Rondon's heart. "Show me the way, Mouse!"

Guluth led Rondon over the grassy hill to a sparkling waterfall that crashed into a deep, clear pond. "We have to swim behind the falls," Guluth explained.

They jumped into the water — Splash! — and swam behind the curtain of falling water. There, Guluth had found a secret cavern. But Guluth knew something Rondon didn't. He knew that in the back of the cave was a narrow, winding tunnel that went deep into the rock.

"The cheese is down there!" Guluth pointed.

Rondon scrambled into the hole, but as he went deeper, the tunnel got tighter and tighter. Rondon was a big, heavy rat, and soon he was wedged tight. He couldn't move forward, and he couldn't move back!

Guluth, being a very small and nimble mouse, easily hopped over Rondon and found a tiny ledge that led back out to the waterfall.

"Mr. Rondon," Guluth said calmly from the ledge. "This is the last time you will ever hurt a neighbor like Harriet. You thought you were great because you were big, but true greatness is being kind."

Guluth popped out of the cave and swam back to the shore. He went back to the village one last time to make sure Harriet was safe in her home.

The other rats, seeing that Rondon was gone and that Guluth was a friend, stopped growling. They realized that if a tiny mouse could be that brave and kind, maybe they could be, too.

Guluth continued on his journey, his little blue satchel a bit lighter, but his heart feeling very full. He looked at the single purple spot on his back and kept walking.

"I am Guluth," he whispered to the wind, "and I have learned that while cheese may fill the belly, kindness is what fills the soul."

The End.
