The Adventures of Guluth

Story 3

The Secret Talent of Julio

On his long travels through Mouseland, Guluth eventually arrived at a very small town. It was so tiny that it only had one local restaurant, but it was famous for having the warmest hearth and the softest cheddar biscuits in the land.

Guluth and a group of fellow travelers were tired and hungry. They had walked through the rain and were looking forward to a quiet dinner. But as they opened the door to the restaurant, they didn't hear the clinking of spoons or the sound of laughter. They heard a loud, booming voice.

"I am Julio!" a large rat shouted, slamming his fist on a table. "I am the protégé of Radataga, and I will stop at nothing! This cheese is mine!"

Julio was scaring all the local mice. He was snatching cheese off their plates and making everyone feel very small. Most of the travelers turned around to leave, but Guluth walked right in. He was a small mouse, and he looked quite calm as he sat at a tiny table near the kitchen.

"Excuse me," Guluth said politely. "We have traveled a long way, and we all need to get dinner. It isn't very nice to eat everyone's cheese before they've had a bite."

Julio laughed, his whiskers twitching. "Little mouse, I don't care what you think. I am a big, scary rat, and I do what I want!"

Julio turned and stomped into the back of the kitchen to find the rest of the cheese wheels. The other mice whispered, "Guluth, be careful! Julio is mean!"

But as Guluth sat there, he heard something strange coming from the kitchen. It wasn't the sound of a mean rat growling. It was a soft, melodic hum — a tune that sounded like the wind through the wheat fields.

Hmm-mmm-mmm, la-dee-da... It was a beautiful, clear voice. Guluth realized that Julio wasn't just shouting; he was singing a gentle song of the old hills. And he was very, very good.

Dad's Question

If a bully is secretly a great singer, do you think they are really mean, or maybe just lonely?

Guluth stood up and walked into the kitchen. He found Julio near the cheese pantry, absentmindedly whistling a tune that sounded like a summer morning. Julio spun around, looking embarrassed. "What do you want, Guluth?"

"Julio," Guluth said, his fur shimmering in the light of the kitchen fire. "I am Guluth, and I have traveled many lands. I have heard the grandest choirs in the mountains, but I have rarely heard a voice as pure as yours."

Julio stopped. "Wait... really?"

"Truly," Guluth said. "I think you have been eating everyone's cheese because you didn't know how to tell them you wanted to sing for them. I have a grand task for you. There is a performance in two days, and we need a star. I think you should be that star."

Julio's eyes went wide. He had spent so much time trying to be a "mean rat" like Radataga told him to be, but he had always wanted to be a singer. "You think they would listen?"

"I know they would," Guluth said.

For the next two days, the town worked together. Guluth helped organize the stage, and the local mice prepared a great feast. But this time, they weren't just serving cheese — they were serving Cheese Milkshakes.

When the night of the performance came, everyone gathered. Julio stepped onto the stage, looking nervous. But then, he began to sing. He sang songs about the stars and the moonlight, and a common song that every mouse and rat knew from their childhood — a song about friendship and home. His voice was so beautiful that the mice and the travelers fell into a trance — they were mesmerized.

After the show, Julio walked up to Guluth. He didn't look mean at all anymore. He looked happy.

"Thank you, Mr. Guluth," Julio said, shaking the small mouse's tiny hand. "Thank you for encouraging me. I feel like I finally found my calling."

Guluth smiled. "I am glad, Julio. You weren't an evil rat; you were just a rat who hadn't found his strength yet. Now, you can be whoever you want to be."

Julio and Guluth parted ways as friends. Guluth adjusted his satchel and headed back out onto the road, the purple birthmark on his shoulder disappearing into the twilight.

"I am Guluth," he whispered, "and every voice deserves to be heard."

The End.
