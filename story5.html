Story 5

The Siege of the Cheddar-Forge

In the heart of Mouseland, there is a very busy place called the Cheddar-Forge. This is where all the mice gather to work together. They take the finest curds and press them into giant, golden wheels with great strength and care. Then, they load those wheels onto tiny carts and deliver them to every mouse's house so that no one ever goes hungry.

Guluth loved working at the Cheddar-Forge. He didn't mind the hard work, and he loved the warm, savory smell of the kitchen. On the menu each week were amazing things: cheddar cheese pies, asiago buns, and even muenster fries!

But one afternoon, while the mice were busy rolling a fresh batch of wheels, they heard a loud stomp, stomp, stomp.

A pack of ten mean rats marched into the yard. They were led by a rat named Marty. Marty was tricky and very greedy.

He stood in the center of the yard and shouted, "Look here, mice! My rat pack is taking all this cheese. We're going to eat every bit of it, and you're never going to have cheese again!"

The other mice were terrified. They started to tremble and hide behind the heavy cheese presses. "Please don't hurt us!" they cried. "You can take it all!"

Dad's Question

There are ten big rats and only one small Guluth. Do you think he can stop them without getting into a fight?

Guluth went into the back pantry of the Forge and pulled out a special, secret ingredient. He began to mix it into a fresh wheel of cheese. It was Sleepy Cheese.

Guluth walked back out to the front of the pack. He put on a worried face — just a little bit of acting — and held out the wheel. "Please, Mr. Marty," Guluth said softly. "Don't take all our cheese. But if you must... here is our very best wheel."

Marty laughed. "I'll take yours next, little mouse!" He snatched the wheel and shared it with his pack. They were so greedy that they ate the whole thing in just a few bites.

Almost instantly, Marty's eyes began to droop. Yawn. One by one, the ten rats fell onto the floor. Snore... Zzzzz. They were fast asleep!

Guluth knew they would only be asleep for a few hours, so he acted quickly. "Quickly, everyone! We have work to do!"

He organized the mice into teams. They brought out ten rolling wagons — one for each rat. Even though the rats were heavy, the mice worked together, pushing and pulling with all their might. They rolled the wagons all the way from the Cheddar-Forge to the edge of the great blue ocean.

When they arrived at the shore, they moved the sleeping rats onto sturdy floating rafts and gave them a gentle push.

The rats drifted further and further away into the deep blue water. A few hours later, Marty was the first to wake up. He rubbed his eyes and looked around, but all he could see was water in every direction.

He saw his pack waking up on their own rafts, looking very confused and very scared.

"They sent us away!" Marty cried.

They floated so far that they were stuck in the middle of the ocean, where they could never bother the Cheddar-Forge again. Back at the yard, all the mice cheered. They threw a giant celebration with parmesan potatoes and Swiss roll bakes.

"Thank you, Guluth!" they shouted. "You saved our food!"

Guluth just smiled and went back to work, helping his neighbors finish their deliveries. He was a humble mouse, but he knew that as long as they were clever and worked together, they would always be safe.

The End.
