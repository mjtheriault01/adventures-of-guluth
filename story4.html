Story 4

The Voyage to the Lost Island

One day, Guluth and his friends decided to go on a grand adventure across the sea. They weren't looking for gold or jewels, because mice don't care much for those things. They were looking for the greatest treasure in the world: the legendary Caribbean Cheese.

They sailed across the blue waves for many days, following an old, crinkly map. One afternoon, while Guluth was sitting at a little seaside café having a cheddar biscuit, a wise old mouse whispered to him.

"If you want the greatest treasure of all, Guluth, you must go to the Lost Island. It is lost because no one knows where it is — but the map will show those with a true heart the way." The old mouse leaned in closer. "But beware. Greed can take you over. If you try to take all the cheese for yourself, you will be lost just like the island."

Guluth nodded solemnly. He knew this cheese wasn't just for him; it was to feed his whole community back home.

They set sail again, pushing through a fog so thick they couldn't see the front of the boat. The mice were scared, but Guluth stayed at the helm, calm and steady.

"Land ho!" shouted a little mouse named Harold.

It was a beautiful island with a giant volcano in the center. But as they rowed their small boats toward the shore, a giant shark leaped from the water! Splash! The mice had to jump out and swim for their lives, barely reaching the sandy beach in time.

Dad's Question

The mice lost their boats and there are sharks in the water. How do you think they feel being stuck on a mystery island?

That night, they heard a rustling in the jungle. Out stepped two scary rats named Raffodil and Kumadil. They had been stuck on the island for years. They wanted the cheese treasure too, but because they were greedy and mean, they could never find it.

Guluth didn't want to fight. Instead, he used his clever mind. He knew rats weren't fond of deep water, so he tied a long, strong rope to a sturdy tree on the beach. He led his friends into the shallow waves, holding onto the rope.

When the rats tried to follow and swim after them, the ocean current began to pull them away. The mice held tight to their rope and pulled themselves back to the safety of the sand, while the rats were washed further down the coast, unable to bother the mice again.

The next morning, the mice traveled through the jungle. They saw monkeys swinging on trapezes and elephants bigger than trees. They saw beautiful parakeets that were bright purple, shiny purple, and even sparkly purple.

Finally, they reached the volcano and climbed for two days until they found the entrance to a deep cavern. Inside sat a tiny turtle named Tertullian.

"I have lived here for five thousand years," Tertullian said in a slow, ancient voice. "You may take the cheese you need to feed your people, but stay true to your hearts. If you touch the forbidden cheese, the cavern will fall."

Inside, they saw it — a brick of cheese bigger than a house! It was magnificent. But a mouse named Mickey saw a different, shiny piece of cheese on a pedestal. He wanted it so badly that he reached out and touched it.

Rumble. Crack. Boom! The cave began to shake. Rocks started falling from the ceiling. The great stone gate began to slide shut.

"Run!" Guluth shouted.

The mice scrambled toward the exit. Guluth reached the gate just as it was about to close. He saw his friend Marvin tripping over a rock behind him. Without a second thought, Guluth braced his small frame against the heavy stone gate, holding it open with all his might.

"Go, Marvin! Go!" Guluth urged.

Marvin squeezed through just in time, but the gate crashed shut. Guluth was trapped inside the dark volcano with only Tertullian the turtle.

"Why did you stay?" the turtle asked. "You are trapped now."

"I am a mouse of my word," Guluth said quietly. "I would rather be trapped here than let my friend be hurt."

Tertullian smiled. "Because you sacrificed yourself for your friends and not for the cheese, you have shown your true heart." The turtle walked to the center of the cave where a magical beam of light — and a waterfall of clear water — poured from the ceiling. "Get on my back, Guluth."

The turtle swam up through the water, carrying Guluth all the way to the top of the volcano and out into the fresh air.

Guluth built a sturdy raft and sailed across the sea. When he finally reached home, his friends were waiting on the docks. They cheered and hugged him, celebrating with the greatest cheese party the world had ever seen.

Guluth sat quietly by the water, happy that everyone was fed and safe. He was just a small mouse, but on the Lost Island, he had shown the strength of a giant.

The End.
